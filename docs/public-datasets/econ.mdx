# Data Inventory
- Federal Reserve Economic Data CSV extracts for UNRATE (940 monthly U.S. unemployment observations; 1948-01 through 2026-04; percent), CPIAUCSL (951 monthly CPI index observations; 1947-01 through 2026-03; 1982-84=100), FEDFUNDS (862 monthly effective federal funds rate observations; 1954-07 through 2026-04; percent), DGS10 (16,787 business-day 10-year Treasury yield observations; 1962-01-02 through 2026-05-06; percent with weekend gaps), and GDP (317 quarterly nominal GDP observations; 1947-01 through 2026-01; billions of chained dollars); each table stores one row per observation_date with provider formats unchanged.
- FHFA all-transactions house price index panels: `hpi_at_state.csv` holds 10,404 quarterly index values across 51 states and D.C. for 1975Q1–2025Q4 (index points, no seasonality adjustment metadata), while `hpi_at_metro.csv` holds 83,640 quarterly rows for 410 CBSAs over the same period with separate raw and seasonally adjusted columns and 16% placeholders (`-`) where FHFA withholds estimates; the county download is absent (only an HTML access page on disk).
- Treasury Fiscal Data exports: `debt_to_penny_full.csv` provides 8,303 daily records of total public debt outstanding, debt held by the public, and intragovernmental holdings from 1993-04-01 through 2026-05-07 in U.S. dollars; `debt_to_penny.csv` retains the original 5,000-row first page truncated at 2013-03-12; `operating_cash_balance.csv` lists 10,000 daily Treasury General Account balance and cash flow records from 2014-06-04 through 2026-05-07 with amounts in millions; paginated `debt_to_penny_page01/02.csv` are also stored as intermediate pulls.
- Treasury par yield curve slice `daily_treasury_yield_curve_2024_2026.csv` captures 588 business-day observations from 2024-01-02 through 2026-05-08 with columns for 1-month through 30-year constant maturity yields (percent); XML source files for 2024–2026 remain alongside the compiled CSV.
- Bureau of Labor Statistics API payloads: `raw/bls_api/CUSR0000SA0.json` holds 132 monthly CPI-U (seasonally adjusted) values for 2015-01–2026-04 (1982-84=100), and `raw/bls_laus/api/state_unemployment_2019_2026.json` contains 51 state series with monthly seasonally adjusted unemployment rates for 2019-01–2026-03 (percent); metadata and footnotes mirror the public API responses.
- Census Building Permits Survey county tables: `co2603c.txt` (current month) and `co2603y.txt` (year-to-date) each include 3,020 March 2026 county-level records with counts, housing units, and valuation totals by 1-unit, 2-unit, 3–4 unit, and 5+ unit structures plus reporting flags.
- Zillow Research smoothed, seasonally adjusted city ZHVI matrix (license review): 21,410 rows keyed by `RegionID` with monthly typical home values in USD from 2000-01 through 2026-03 across cities, metros, and counties; redistribution awaits legal clearance.
- Census Bureau microdata archives stored as-delivered ZIPs: ACS 2024 1-year PUMS (`csv_pus.zip` and `csv_hus.zip` containing person and housing CSVs ~1.2 GB each plus README), CPS January 2026 basic monthly (`jan26pub.zip` with fixed-width `.dat`), and AHS 2023 National PUF (`AHS_2023_National_PUF_v1.1_CSV.zip` with household, person, mortgage, and project tables); files remain compressed with no extracted derivatives on disk.
- Bureau of Economic Analysis CAINC1 ZIP (`raw/bea/CAINC1.zip`) bundles 55 CSV tables including nationwide and state personal income metrics (1969–2024) plus definitions and footnotes; archives are unexpanded pending checksum and schema inspection.
- Bank for International Settlements locational banking statistics bulk archive (`raw/bis/WS_LBS_D_PUB_csv_col.zip`, license review) provides quarterly cross-border position CSVs through 2025Q4 for reporting/counterparty combinations under BIS terms; contents remain compressed.
- OECD composite leading indicator SDMX payload (`raw/oecd/MEI_CLI_USA.json`, license review) stores the May 2026 release of U.S. CLI series with monthly observation arrays embedded in SDMX CompactData; legal approval required before sharing.
- NBER business cycle chronology HTML snapshot (`raw/nber/cyclesmain.html`, license review) captures U.S. recession peak and trough dates spanning 1854–2020 for reference pending redistribution clearance.
- Blockers and artefacts on disk: BLS ATUS (`raw/bls/atus/atusresp-2024.zip`), CEX (`raw/bls/cex/intrvw24.zip.error.txt`), HUD SAFMR/CHAS info files, Redfin market trends CSV, IMF IFS request, and several BLS LAUS county files resolve to HTML or error text rather than data; `.remote-agent/` automation workspaces (≈29k files, 10.9 GB) remain present and should be excluded from canonical distributions.
